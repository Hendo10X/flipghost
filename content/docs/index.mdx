Flipghost is a flipbook animator that runs in your browser. You draw each frame
by hand, trace the motion between them with onion skinning, and export the
result as a looping GIF or an MP4. There is nothing to install and nothing to
set up.

This page gets you to a finished loop. The rest of the docs go deeper on each
part of the workshop.

## Your first loop

Open the [workshop](/workshop) and follow along. The whole thing takes about a
minute, and finishing something small teaches more than reading the rest of
these docs will.

### 1. Draw something simple

Pick the brush with <kbd>B</kbd> and draw a circle near the left of the canvas.
Do not make it good. A wobbly circle is the correct amount of effort here.

### 2. Duplicate the frame

Press the duplicate button in the timeline. You now have two identical frames.
Duplicating rather than adding a blank frame is the normal way to work, because
most frames are mostly the same as the one before them.

### 3. Move the circle

You are now on frame 2. Erase the circle with <kbd>E</kbd> and redraw it
slightly to the right. The faint red ghost behind it is frame 1, showing you
exactly where you were. That ghost is onion skinning, and it is the reason this
tool exists.

### 4. Repeat

Duplicate, erase, redraw a little further along. Do this until the circle has
crossed the canvas. Six frames is plenty.

### 5. Play it

Press <kbd>Space</kbd>. The circle moves. That is animation, and you have now
done all of it. Everything else is a refinement of these five steps.

<Callout>
  If the motion looks jerky, you probably moved the circle too far between
  frames. If it looks slow, you moved it too little. That relationship between
  distance and time is called spacing, and it is most of the craft.
</Callout>

## Requirements

Flipghost runs on any modern browser. The workshop needs the canvas and the
timeline on screen at the same time, so it is available on tablets and larger
screens but not on phones.

A stylus is not required, but it is the best way to use this. See
[pressure](/docs/canvas#pressure) for what changes when you draw with a pen.
